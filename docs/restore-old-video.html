How to restore old video from tapes, DVDs, and camcorders

The best old-video restoration workflow is simple: start with a safe cleanup pass, inspect a short preview, then upscale only after the footage is stable and clean.

Common old-video problems

Old SD footage can be interlaced, noisy, compressed, color-shifted, soft, or shaky. Trying to upscale first often makes those flaws more visible.

A practical restoration order

  1. Deinterlace when the source came from tape, DVD, or broadcast video.
  2. Denoise and clean compression artifacts.
  3. Sharpen lightly so faces and edges stay natural.
  4. Upscale to a modern delivery size such as 1080p.
  5. Use AI detail restoration only after checking a short preview.
