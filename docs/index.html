System requirements

Phoenix is designed to run locally. Faster GPUs help, but the app should make the safe path clear before you start a long export.

Minimum

Modern Windows or Linux PC, 8 GB RAM, enough disk space for temporary video files, and FFmpeg-compatible media.

Recommended

16 GB RAM, recent AMD/Intel/NVIDIA GPU, SSD storage, and short test exports before processing long tapes. Windows builds include DirectML for broad GPU support.

Generative AI

NVIDIA GPU recommended. 8 GB VRAM can run Lite profiles; heavier modes need more VRAM and patience.

FAQ

Is my footage uploaded to the internet?

Never. Phoenix runs 100% on your computer. Your files never leave your machine.

Can Phoenix replace Topaz for old tapes?

For many VHS, broadcast SD, and home archive jobs, Phoenix is a simpler and cheaper local option. Topaz remains the choice for high-end professional pipelines.

What makes Phoenix different from online AI video enhancers?

Phoenix runs locally. That means wedding videos, family tapes, school events, and private archives do not need to be uploaded to a third-party server.

What video formats work?

MP4, MOV, MKV, AVI, and most common formats from cameras, DVDs, and VHS capture cards.

Is generative restoration the default?

No. Standard restoration is the safer default. Generative Lite is an advanced option for supported hardware and short preview-driven workflows.

How do I activate Pro?

Subscribe on Gumroad, check your email for a PHX- license key, open Phoenix → Account → paste the key.

Do AMD GPUs work?

Yes for the standard ONNX restoration path on Windows. Phoenix packages ONNX Runtime DirectML so many AMD, Intel, and NVIDIA DirectX 12 GPUs can accelerate local restoration. Generative Lite remains NVIDIA-focused.

Do I need a powerful GPU?

A GPU speeds things up, but Phoenix works on everyday laptops too. Processing runs locally either way.
